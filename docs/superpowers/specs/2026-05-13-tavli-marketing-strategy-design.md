# Tavli — Marketing & Go-to-Market Strategy (v2)

> *"Find your table in Romania."*
> Brand: **Tavli** (codename `masaro` in repo). Domain: tavli.ro.
> Date: 2026-05-13. Author: founder + Claude (brainstorm session).
> v1 → v2 revision in same session: shifted from editorial-led + AI-concierge ("The Curator") to SaaS-led + editorial-as-distribution ("The Underminer + hybrid quality bar").

## TL;DR

**Tavli is a restaurant-reservation SaaS that undercuts ialoc.ro on price (~€30–€40/mo vs ~€90/mo) with a more beautiful and more functional product, backed by editorial-quality trilingual content/SEO that drives diners to Tavli-listed restaurants — proving the subscription's value with real bookings, not promises.**

Playbook reference: **Resy / Tock / SevenRooms** (restaurant SaaS first, consumer surface as customer acquisition for the SaaS), with **content quality cribbed from Eater / Infatuation** as a free, compounding brand asset.

**Constraints we are designing inside:**
- Budget: ~€100 / year of cash. Founder time + AI = the actual budget.
- Timeline: no fixed launch; ~24 months of patient compounding.
- Team: founder (engineer, Bucharest-based, writes well) + wife (fluent in German for DE spot-check).
- Product surface: consumer reservations + corporate events (both shipping).
- Languages: RO + EN + DE from day one, parallel-written, not translated.

**The three motions, ranked by leverage:**
1. **Restaurant SaaS sales** — direct founder-led B2B sales, target 30–60 restaurants signed in year one, undercut ialoc on price + product depth
2. **Editorial content engine** — trilingual long-form content that ranks on long-tail queries, drives diner bookings to Tavli-listed restaurants, and serves as a B2B sales proof point
3. **Corporate-events as a premium subscription tier** — Tavli Pro at €60/mo routes corporate event leads to opt-in restaurants

## Why this strategy and not the alternative we considered

We explicitly considered and rejected the "Curator" strategy in the same brainstorm session:

| | Curator (Eater/Infatuation) | Underminer (Resy/Tock/SevenRooms) |
|---|---|---|
| Center of gravity | Media brand → marketplace | SaaS → discovery surface |
| Who pays | Diners + corporates | Restaurants (subscription) |
| First revenue | Month 6–9 | Month 1–2 |
| Y1 ceiling | €5–15k total | €15–30k MRR run-rate |
| Engineering | AI concierge (real complexity) | Restaurant portal + migration tools |
| Year-3 ceiling | Higher (brand unbounded) | Lower (TAM-bounded) |
| Year-3 floor | Lower (could plateau) | Higher (MRR predictable) |
| Defensibility | Brand ialoc can't replicate | Switching cost + product depth |
| Ialoc's defense | Almost none | Can drop price or copy features |

**Why we picked the Underminer for this founder + these constraints:**
- Faster path to revenue — MRR matters when there's no investor runway
- €100 budget genuinely sufficient (sales is time, not money)
- Engineer-founder skill match (the product IS the offering)
- Removes the AI capability bet
- Concrete and measurable: closed restaurants and MRR aren't ambiguous

**Why we keep editorial-quality content** (not pure programmatic SEO):
- Good editorial is a free, compounding brand asset on top of SaaS revenue
- Editorial content is ialoc's structural weakness (they can't be opinionated)
- It's the sales proof point that closes restaurants: *"800 organic visits to your competitor's Tavli page last month — your page is next."*
- Mass AI content remains an explicit anti-pattern (Google quality signals, voice integrity)

## The brand promise

> **Tavli helps Romanian restaurants run a better reservation business — and helps diners find places worth going.**

Two audiences, one brand, internally consistent:
- **To restaurants:** "We're a better, cheaper, prettier reservation system than ialoc, with bookings flowing in from our trilingual content surface."
- **To diners:** "We've eaten here. We'll tell you the truth. Book the good ones."

Voice attributes (the rails AI must stay inside):
- **Opinionated**, with reasoning. Not "this is a nice place"; "the bread is worth ordering twice, the wine list rewards risk, skip the dessert."
- **Direct.** No travel-writing fluff.
- **First-person (collective).** "We've eaten here."
- **Trilingual but not translated.** RO + EN + DE are each parallel originals.
- **Service-oriented.** Every consumer piece answers: *should I go, and what should I order/avoid?*
- **B2B-honest.** Sales pages name the price and the features without coyness.

## The three motions in detail

### Motion 1: Restaurant SaaS sales (the primary motion)

**Goal:** 30–60 paying restaurants by month 12; €1,500–€2,500 MRR.

**Pricing tiers:**
- **Tavli Base — €30/mo or €35/mo** (final TBD): reservation system, trilingual venue page, basic analytics. ~60–65% undercut of ialoc.
- **Tavli Pro — €60/mo:** Base plus routed corporate event leads, advanced analytics, featured placement in editorial guides where editorially honest, marketing tools.
- **First 2 months free** for any restaurant migrating from ialoc.
- **Annual prepay discount** (10%) — pulls cash forward and reduces churn.

**Target buyer:** owner or general manager of independent Bucharest restaurants currently on ialoc, especially those in price tier 3–4 (mid-to-upper) where margins justify caring about reservation tools. Chains and very-small joints are secondary.

**Sales motion (founder-led, in-person, year one):**
1. Visit in person at off-peak hours (15:00–17:00, Tue–Thu).
2. Ask for the owner or manager directly. Avoid hostess pitches.
3. Open with the proof: show their *competitor's* Tavli page on phone, with traffic numbers and a reservation count.
4. Five-minute demo of the restaurant portal vs ialoc's portal, side-by-side on phone.
5. Pitch: *"€30 a month, we migrate you from ialoc in 30 minutes, first 2 months free, you keep ialoc running in parallel for a month if you want."*
6. Close in the room, or set a 7-day follow-up. No "we'll think about it" without a date attached.
7. Sign on the spot via mobile-friendly contract form.

**Pace target:** 5 in-person visits per week → 1–2 closes per week by month 3 → 2–3 closes per week by month 6.

**Switching-cost mitigation (this is engineering work):**
- ialoc data import tool (CSV or scrape, depending on what's exportable)
- Pre-filled website widget code (just paste-and-replace)
- 30-minute white-glove migration session for every new restaurant
- Parallel run support for first month

### Motion 2: Editorial content engine

**Output target:** 3 pieces/week trilingual, year-round. ~150 pieces year one. ~300 by month 18.

**Format mix:**
- **Restaurant deep-dives (50%)** — the SEO + sales-proof feedstock. Each one ranks on long-tail queries AND becomes a screenshot in a sales pitch.
- **Themed lists (20%)** — "12 best terraces in Bucharest," "Where chefs eat on their day off," "Romania's tasting menus ranked." High SEO value, high shareability.
- **Interviews / reportage (15%)** — chef profiles, sommelier conversations, scene reporting. Builds brand depth.
- **News + openings (10%)** — what's new, what's closing, what's worth knowing this week.
- **Newsletter (5%, weekly)** — the most undeniably human artifact; never AI-written; goes out Thursdays.

**Trilingual production:**
- RO and EN at full parity from week 1.
- DE added in week 5–6 once bilingual pipeline is stable. Wife spot-checks every DE piece on Fridays.
- Each language is a parallel original, not a translation. AI parallel-writes from the angle + verdict, not from the RO text.

**AI pipeline (discipline that protects the moat):**
1. Founder sets angle + verdict in 2 sentences. AI never sets the angle.
2. AI does: research brief, draft scaffold, fact assembly, RO/EN/DE parallel writing, SEO metadata, style consistency check.
3. Founder injects: first-hand experience, an original photo, a specific line, the take.
4. Every piece passes: *would a Bucharest reader recognize this as written by a real person who's been there?*
5. Weekly voice-anchor read every Friday — if it doesn't sound like Tavli, prompts get tuned.

**Anti-patterns explicitly excluded:**
- Mass directory pages for every venue
- Auto-generated listicles without first-hand experience
- AI-written newsletters
- Programmatic SEO ("best restaurants in [Bucharest sector X]" doorway pages)

### Motion 3: Corporate-events premium tier

**Reframed from v1:** no longer a parallel motion; integrated into the SaaS revenue model as a premium subscription tier.

**How it works:**
- Restaurants opt in to **Tavli Pro (€60/mo)** to receive corporate event leads.
- Founder runs a small B2B-events outreach motion on LinkedIn (~10 contacts/week to HR/EA/office managers at UiPath, eMag, Bitdefender, Endava, Big-4, banks).
- Inbound corporate inquiries are routed to 2–3 Pro restaurants matching the brief. Restaurant closes, Tavli takes nothing extra beyond the subscription — the lead routing is the value.
- (Optional v2 later: take a small commission on closed corporate events; deferred until subscription motion is stable.)

**Year 1 corporate targets:**
- 5+ Tavli Pro restaurants by month 6
- 10+ by month 12
- 1 routed corporate inquiry / week by month 9

## The functional advantages over ialoc (draft — to be validated by founder)

The SaaS pitch needs 3–5 named, concrete advantages over ialoc's restaurant portal. Drafted from what's been built / in flight in the repo; the founder must confirm, edit, and add:

| Candidate advantage | Why it matters to a restaurateur | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Corporate / private-events handling** | ialoc has nothing here; you have a full module shipping | ✅ in flight |
| **Verified reservation reviews** (only show-up diners can review) | Kills fake reviews; restaurateurs love this | ✅ planned |
| **Modern mobile-first reservation flow** | Higher diner conversion = more bookings = ROI on subscription | ✅ shipping |
| **Trilingual venue pages (RO/EN/DE)** | ialoc is RO-only; you reach expats, tourists, German speakers | ✅ shipping |
| **QR menu codes** (in-venue feature) | Diner scans → menu → next reservation; loop-closer | ✅ planned |
| **Modern GDPR/ANPC compliance** | Lower legal risk; visible "done right" signal | ✅ shipped |
| **Structured cancellation reasons** (better no-show analytics) | Better data → fewer no-shows → real money saved | ✅ shipped |
| **Google Maps + Links integration** | Better local SEO for the restaurant | ✅ shipped |
| **Cleaner partner portal UX** | Less staff training time, fewer support tickets | ✅ shipping |

**Action for founder:** sit with this list, validate / edit / expand. Pick the **top 3 to lead the sales pitch with.** Those are the spine of every restaurant conversation in weeks 5–12.

## Scoreboard — the only metrics that matter

Three north-star metrics. Everything else is noise.

1. **Paying restaurant count + MRR** (primary). Updated weekly. The business is alive if this compounds.
2. **Reservations from organic editorial pages** (booking funnel from blog/list page → confirmed reservation). Proves the content engine actually feeds the SaaS thesis. Target: 50/month by month 12.
3. **Tavli Pro restaurant count** (corporate-events tier conversions). Premium tier penetration; signals whether corporate-events is a real revenue layer or window dressing.

Explicitly NOT tracked as north-star: total pageviews, Instagram followers, vanity press mentions, total restaurant count (only *paying* count matters), unpaid restaurant "listings."

## Risks and mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| ialoc drops price to match | High | Medium | Win on product depth, not just price; lock in annual contracts |
| Switching cost > price savings | Medium | High | Frictionless migration tooling; white-glove onboarding; 2 months free; parallel-run support |
| Restaurants churn at month 3 because bookings don't appear | Medium | Fatal | Consumer SEO must hit 50 organic reservations/mo by month 12; if not, strategy is failing — re-evaluate |
| Founder sales bandwidth caps at ~30 closes/year | Medium | High | Plan for it; don't promise 100 restaurants; refine sales process so close rate improves over time |
| AI content slop creeps in, kills voice | Medium | High | Friday voice-anchor read; mandatory first-hand input; mandatory original photo; wife spot-checks DE |
| Google penalty on trilingual AI-assisted content | Low | High | Strong experience signals; original photos; author bios; first-hand language; monitor Search Console |
| Build a SaaS no diner wants to book through | Medium | Fatal | The content engine is what prevents this — diner-facing pages must be genuinely useful |
| Funded competitor enters (TheFork doubles down) | Low–Medium | Medium | Speed + relationships are the moat; year-1 install base + content is not easily replicable |

## What we explicitly de-prioritize

- **AI concierge** — removed entirely. Too complex for current constraints; revisit only if business is healthy at month 18+.
- **Paid advertising** in year one. €100 doesn't move the needle; sales is the channel.
- **Geographic expansion** beyond Bucharest until ~month 12. Sales + content depth in one city first.
- **Hiring** until MRR justifies it. Probably ~€3k MRR threshold.
- **Mobile native apps** in year one. Web mobile-first is enough.
- **Vanity press chasing.** Accept organic press; don't pitch.
- **Investor conversations** until month 12 unless inbound on healthy terms.
