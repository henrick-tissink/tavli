## Two tiers. One promise.

Tavli is a reservation platform built like the restaurants that use it — in service of the diner, in service of the room, in service of the people running it. Two subscriptions, monthly or annual. **No per-cover fees. No setup fees. No contract you can't walk away from.**

If you're running an independent restaurant in Romania, **Tavli at €30/month** is everything you need. If you also do private events, group dining, or you want to grow your regulars instead of just managing them, **Tavli Pro at €60/month** adds the corporate-events channel and a full marketing suite.

That's the offer. Two tiers, the first three months free for every new restaurant, and the founder personally setting you up before you ever pay us a euro.

## Tavli — €30/month

*A complete reservation system for one independent restaurant.*

### Take reservations on every surface
- **Unlimited reservations.** Never a per-cover fee.
- A booking widget that drops into your site in two lines of code — and looks like part of *your* brand, not part of ours
- Mobile-first flow: three taps on a phone, no app for diners to install
- Calendar and day view of every booking, on your phone or your laptop
- Modify, cancel, mark no-show — by you or by the diner via secure link
- Structured cancellation reasons, so your no-show data is data, not anecdotes
- Timezone-aware: no booking the past at 23:59 from a different country

### Speak to diners in their language
- Automated confirmations and 24-hour reminders in **Romanian, English, and German**
- Self-serve modify and cancel links — fewer phone calls to your host stand
- Allergy, occasion, seating preferences captured at booking and visible the moment a diner walks in

### A venue page worth showing off
- Trilingual page (RO / EN / DE) — each a parallel original, not a translation. Speak to expats, tourists, and the Romanian diaspora in the voice they actually read in.
- Up to 20 photos, up to 2 menus (PDF or structured items, switchable by service)
- QR menu codes for your table tents
- Google Maps and Google Business sync — local SEO out of the box

### Reviews that mean something
- Only diners with a checked-in reservation can review your restaurant
- No fake reviews. No anonymous trolling. Your reputation, defended by reservations data.

### Numbers you can act on
- 12 months of booking history, exportable to CSV any time
- Covers per service, no-show rate, party-size mix, cancellation-reason breakdown
- A weekly summary email — Sunday night, on the desk Monday morning

### Built to run
- Up to 5 staff accounts (owner, manager, hosts)
- 1 location
- GDPR + ANPC compliant out of the box
- Email + chat support, next-business-day response

## Tavli Pro — €60/month

*Everything in Tavli, plus the corporate-events channel and a marketing suite for restaurants that want to grow regulars, not just manage covers.*

### Corporate & private events *(the headline reason to upgrade)*

The channel most independent restaurants don't have a way to plug into. We do the B2B outreach to HR teams and executive assistants across Bucharest's largest employers. Qualified inquiries get routed to opt-in Pro restaurants matched on cuisine, capacity, and price point.

- Private-rooms and buyout module: multi-room availability, custom event request flow
- Deposits at booking, Stripe-integrated
- Event-specific terms, capacity rules, lead-time minimums
- **Inbound corporate-events lead routing** — opt in, get matched
- Invoiceable line items + downloadable invoice PDFs

### The marketing suite

Built for the work you don't have time to do. Three channels, six set-and-forget campaigns, segmentation by six dimensions, frequency-capped so you never spam your own diners.

- **Channels:** email, SMS via Twilio, WhatsApp Business, in-confirmation upsells
- **Automated campaigns** *(set once, run forever):*
  - Post-visit thank-you + review request (2 hours after check-out, verified diners only)
  - Pre-arrival reminder (day before, with menu preview and parking note)
  - Birthday / anniversary (7 days before, optional offer)
  - Lapsed-diner reactivation (60 / 120 / 180 days)
  - No-show follow-up ("sorry we missed you, here's €10 off your next reservation")
  - Welcome series for first-time diners
- **Segmentation:** visit recency, visit frequency, party size, service preference, occasion tags, acquisition channel
- **One-off campaigns:** template library (winter menu, themed night, off-peak fill, holiday menus) + a simple builder
- **List building:** booking-flow consent, QR table-tent signup, import with consent attestation
- **Bundled allowance:** 1,000 emails + 250 SMS + 250 WhatsApp / month. Transparent overage thereafter. **Frequency cap of 4 messages/diner/month across all channels — no diner ever gets spammed, no restaurant ever gets a surprise bill.**
- **Analytics:** opens, clicks, conversions, bookings attributed to a campaign

Full v1 scope and what's deferred to v1.5: see [Appendix A](#appendix-a--marketing-suite-v1-full-scope).

### Featured in Tavli editorial
- First right to be featured in Tavli's editorial guides — where editorially honest. We don't write nice things about food we wouldn't eat, and that integrity is what makes the editorial worth being in.
- Photo rights to use Tavli's photography of your venue in your own marketing

### Numbers that drive decisions

- Lead-time distribution: how far ahead your diners book

- 4-week rolling cover forecast

### Built for more
- Unlimited staff accounts
- Up to 3 locations included; additional locations €15/mo each
- Unlimited photos, unlimited menus, video hero on the venue page
- Custom widget styling (full CSS control, not just presets)

### Support that picks up the phone
- Same-business-day response

## The setup — what we do for you in your first three months free

Three months free isn't a free trial. It's a setup window. The founder personally invests time in your launch before you ever pay us a euro.

1. **30-minute white-glove migration.** We move your reservations, your diner list, and your settings out of whatever you're using now and into Tavli. One sitting.
2. **Page-and-photos session.** The founder visits your restaurant, photographs your dishes and dining room with proper equipment, and writes your trilingual venue page on the spot. You approve before publish.
3. **30-minute staff training.** Your hosts and managers walk through the partner portal with us until they're comfortable.
4. **Parallel run for 30 days.** Keep your old system live alongside Tavli for the first month. Switch over when you're ready, not when we tell you to.
5. **(Pro only) First three campaigns set up live.** We sit down with you and design your welcome series, your first lapsed-diner reactivation, and your first themed campaign — together, on your data.

After 90 days, the card you put on file at signup is charged. Until then, you owe us nothing.

## The promises we make

These aren't marketing lines. They're contract terms. We will not strike any of them out.

- **No per-cover fees, ever.** If we ever change that pricing model in the future, your current contract grandfathers you out forever.
- **You own your data.** Cancel any time and we hand you a full CSV export of every diner, every booking, every review, every campaign. No data hostage situation.
- **Annual prepay is refundable pro-rata.** If you prepay annually and decide at month 4 it isn't working, we refund the unused months. No questions.
- **No annual lock-in unless you choose it.** Monthly billing is the default. Annual prepay is for the 2-months-free discount, not for keeping you stuck.
- **Cancellation is one click in the product.** No support ticket, no retention call, no "we'll process this in 7-10 business days."
- **One free trial per legal entity.** Card on file at signup. Billing starts day 91. No surprise charges before then.

## Appendix A — Marketing suite v1 (full scope)

The premium reason a busy restaurant moves from Tavli to Tavli Pro after corporate events. Designed for ~4–6 weeks of engineering. Cost-conservative for both Tavli and the restaurant.

### Channels (v1)
- **Email** — via Resend or Postmark, effectively free at our scale
- **SMS** — via Twilio EU, ANPC-compliant opt-in language
- **WhatsApp Business templates** — via Twilio, pre-approved templates only *(scope question — see Appendix D)*
- **In-confirmation upsells** — embedded promo blocks in the booking-confirmation email (the highest-engagement surface a restaurant owns)

### Six automated triggered campaigns
- Post-visit thank-you + review request (verified diners only)
- Pre-arrival reminder (day before)
- Birthday / anniversary (7 days before)
- Lapsed-diner reactivation (60 / 120 / 180 days)
- No-show follow-up
- Welcome series for first-time diners

### Segmentation (v1, six dimensions)
- Visit recency (active / lapsed / dormant)
- Visit frequency (one-timer / occasional / regular)
- Party size typical range
- Service preference (lunch / dinner / brunch)
- Special-occasion tags (birthday, anniversary)
- Acquisition channel (your widget / Tavli venue page / editorial / corporate)

### One-off campaigns (v1)
- Template library: winter menu, new chef, themed night, off-peak fill, holiday menus
- Simple builder: pick template → segment → schedule → send

### List building (v1)
- Booking-flow consent (GDPR-clean, separate consent per channel)
- QR table-tent signup
- Import with consent attestation

### Bundled allowance + transparent overage
- **Included in Pro:** 1,000 emails + 250 SMS + 250 WhatsApp / month
- **Overage:** €0.06 per SMS, €0.03 per WhatsApp, email free
- **Frequency cap:** max 4 messages per diner per month across all channels

### Compliance (table stakes, not sellable)
- GDPR consent record per diner per channel
- ANPC-compliant SMS opt-in language
- Quiet hours: no SMS/WhatsApp before 10:00 or after 21:00 in diner's local time
- One-click unsubscribe + org-wide suppression list

### Analytics (v1)
- Per-campaign opens, clicks, conversions
- Bookings attributed to a campaign (campaign_id on booking)

### Explicitly deferred to v1.5 (post-launch within 90 days)
- Drag-drop email builder, A/B testing, send-time optimization
- Off-peak flash promos, standing off-peak discounts, waitlist alerts
- Loyalty / referrals / "regular" status auto-tagging
- Photo / UGC moderation, Google review nudges
- Channel-mix ROI dashboards

### Explicitly out of scope
- Push notifications (no diner app)
- Social-media scheduling (wrong problem, wrong buyer)
- POS-integrated revenue attribution (v2 minimum)

## Appendix D — Decisions to lock

Each one has a recommended default. Just confirm or override.

### Marketing suite scope
1. **WhatsApp Business in v1?** *Recommended: yes.* RO leverage is enormous and WhatsApp uses the same Twilio integration as SMS. Engineering scope: ~5 extra days (Meta Business verification + template approval). The clean-cut alternative is "email + SMS in v1, WhatsApp in v1.5."
2. **Cost model A or B?** *Recommended: A — bundled allowance + transparent overage.* Restaurant-friendly, predictable, frequency cap prevents runaway. B (pass-through) is less risky for us but loads more friction onto restaurants.
3. **Loyalty / referrals in v1 or v1.5?** *Recommended: v1.5.* Complex, rarely the buyer's first ask, sticky once installed but doesn't make-or-break Pro launch.

### Pricing & packaging
4. **Pro feature scope vs ship date.** *Recommended: ship Pro at W8 with the "can ship with coming soon" list above*, rather than delaying launch. Restaurants close on corporate events and marketing suite, not on Stripe-integrated deposits.
5. **Per-additional-location price.** *Recommended: €15/mo per additional location, no hard cap.* Sanity-check against a real 3–5 location target during W3-W4 sales visits.
6. **Stripe deposits architecture.** *Recommended: Stripe Connect, each restaurant has its own Stripe account.* Cleaner regulatory story, no licensing exposure for Tavli. Each restaurant signs the standard Stripe terms.
7. **Currency presentation on the page.** *Recommended: EUR primary, RON subtext.* EUR anchors against international SaaS pricing; RON shows local commitment.
8. **Pricing-page launch date.** *Recommended: public from day one of W1.* Signals confidence. Quote-on-request signals weakness.
9. **TVA presentation.** *Recommended: "All prices + TVA" stated once, prominently.* B2B norm in Romania; don't pretend prices include TVA.
10. **Multi-currency invoicing for corporate events.** *Recommended: restaurant invoices the corporate client directly in RON; Tavli's role ends at the lead handoff.* No FX exposure for Tavli.

### Tier naming
11. **Tavli Pro or something more hospitality-coded?** *Recommended: keep "Tavli Pro."* "Pro" is universally understood in B2B SaaS and the founder's sales motion benefits from instant clarity. Alternatives considered: "Tavli Hospitality," "Tavli Reserve" — both more lyrical but require a half-second of explanation that hurts close rate.

## Appendix E — Bold options we're considering (not yet committed)

These are the moves that would make the offer remarkable but carry real cost or risk. Each one has my recommendation but they're all genuinely open.

### Option 1 — Corporate-events soft money-back guarantee on Pro
> *"If Tavli Pro doesn't deliver at least one qualified corporate-events lead in your first 6 months, we refund your Pro premium back to Base pricing (€30 instead of €60) for those months."*

**Why it's incredible:** removes the buying risk on Pro entirely. Signals founder confidence in the corporate motion. Forces us to actually make the motion work. "Qualified" defined contractually as: meets the restaurant's stated capacity, cuisine, and price-point criteria.

**Cost ceiling:** if 30 Pro restaurants don't get a lead in 6 months, refund = (€60 - €30) × 6 × 30 = **€5,400**. Capped, contained.

**Risk:** if the corporate motion is slower to spin up than planned, this triggers. Mitigated by waiting until W12 to enable the guarantee (i.e., the first cohort of Pro restaurants gets the guarantee starting W12, after we've proven we can source leads).

**Recommendation:** **yes, but enable from W12 onwards**, not W8.

### Option 2 — Tavli photography rights for Pro restaurants' own marketing
> *Pro restaurants get the rights to use Tavli's photography of their venue in their own marketing channels (Instagram, their website, print menus), in addition to it appearing on their Tavli venue page.*

**Why it's incredible:** most restaurants have terrible photography. Tavli's photos (founder-shot in the setup session, edited well) become an asset they can use everywhere. Costs us nothing extra — we're shooting the photos anyway.

**Risk:** none. Pure upside.

**Recommendation:** **yes**, write it into the contract, mention it in the pricing page.

### Option 3 — Founder-led page-and-photos session for *every* new signup, not just Pro
> *Currently positioned as part of "the setup." Should the founder-led photo session apply to Base too, or only to Pro?*

**Why it might be incredible:** makes every restaurant's launch feel premium, kills the perception that Base is the "lite" tier, and generates editorial-quality assets even from Base restaurants for the content engine.

**Cost:** founder time. ~90 min per restaurant × 30-60 restaurants in Y1 = 45-90 hours. Significant.

**Risk:** founder time is the hardest constraint. Could shift to "page-and-photos for Pro, page-only (no photos) for Base, photos available as a paid add-on for Base at €100 one-time."

**Recommendation:** **yes for Base too**, but tighten the scope to 60 min on-site instead of 90, and accept the founder-time cost as worth it for the editorial assets and the close rate boost.

### Option 4 — "Anniversary perk" auto-renewal
> *Every restaurant that's been on Tavli for 12 months gets one month free on their anniversary, automatically applied to the next bill.*

**Why it might be incredible:** retention move, makes restaurants feel valued, costs ~8% of their annual revenue to Tavli (one month free), turns "stayed because they're locked in" into "stayed because we noticed."

**Cost:** ~8% of MRR for any restaurant past month 12.

**Recommendation:** **maybe** — defer the decision to month 10 of operation, when we know more about churn.

### Option 5 — Trilingual venue page written by Tavli's founder, included in setup
> *Currently in "the setup." Worth restating loudly: the founder personally writes the trilingual venue page in editorial voice as part of onboarding. This is unusual enough to be a standalone feature.*

**Why it's incredible:** trilingual editorial copy normally costs €300-500/page if commissioned. Most restaurants have nothing of the kind. Founder time is the budget, and the page doubles as a content asset for Tavli.

**Cost:** ~2 hours of founder time per restaurant.

**Recommendation:** **already in the setup, just make sure the pricing-page copy makes a meal of it.**
